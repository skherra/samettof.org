h1: 🇳🇱 Statia

country-desc: |
  **[Statia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sint_Eustatius){: target="_blank"}**, or Sint Eustatius in the Dutch Antilles, a short flight from St Martin, is a tiny, peaceful Caribbean island where everyone says hello to everyone <i class="fa fa-smile-o"></i>.

  It's a paradise for wrecks, old ones and recently sunk ones alike, and for the spiny lobsters you run into on every dive. Statia is full of dive sites, each as beautiful as the last, but our favorite by far&nbsp;: the **Chien Tong** at night, a real Turtle paradise&nbsp;!

  **<i class="fa fa-mail-forward"></i>**{: .accent-color} The best of&nbsp;: **Chien Tong, Stenapa**.

dive-shop: |
  In February 2016 and December 2023.
  {: .visited-in}

  **[Scubaqua <i class="fa fa-heart"></i>](https://www.scubaqua.com/){: target="_blank"}**&nbsp;: We first discovered this small dive club, run by Ingrid and Meno, in 2016. A great, always-smiling and funny team, a pleasure to dive with all of them, not forgetting the singing dog.

  In 2023, a new team with their partners Marika and Mike. Still warm and professional, we head out for two dives in the morning, with a stop back at base in between. The clubhouse is really pleasant, locals even come by for a drink. The adjoining beach is popular with snorkelers.

  Two very comfortable boats, Nitrox dives available, and a night dive scheduled during the week.

accommodation: |
  In December 2023.
  {: .visited-in}

  **[The old Gin House](https://www.theoldginhouse.com/){: target="_blank"}**  right on the waterfront, a stone's throw from the dive club and the bars and restaurants. It just changed owners, the rooms are spacious, with a small terrace by the pool (which could use a renovation) or a sea view. Being right on the road, the hotel stays noisy from 6:30am to 10pm. The restaurant, with a nice view, is passable and fairly pricey.

  In February 2016.
  {: .visited-in}

  **[Statia Lodge](https://www.statialodge.com/){: target="_blank"}**   up in the hills, a 10-minute scooter ride from the harbor, simple and clean bungalows. A nice pool with a view over St Kitts. By 2023, the lodges could really use a serious renovation.

to-see: |
  In December 2023 and February 2016.
  {: .visited-in}

  You come here to dive, but **the town**, with its pretty houses, the tiny museum and the fort are worth a look. You can hike the **Quill** volcano, with panoramic views and lush vegetation (allow at least half a day).

  The most improbable side trip, over in St Martin&nbsp;: the **Driftwood Boat Bar** on the beach behind the airport to watch planes land&nbsp;! A 5-minute walk from the airport.

  text: |
    Descent along the line onto the Charly, an imposing 100-meter-long wreck, a very impressive dark mass. Visibility is excellent at 40 meters, we come across a large barracuda standing guard, and a school of playful jacks. Tof has BCD trouble, it's leaking, and she has to do everything by lung volume to stay stable. We ascend over the top of this cable-layer, turtles circling below, it's mating season. We ascend the line, a light current making us drift a little.
- author: Sam
  place: Chien Tong
  date: 01/03/2016
  text: |
    Our most beautiful night dive&nbsp;: descent onto this trawler, we come across turtles full of air settling under the cables, ropes or in the ship's openings and beams. We stop counting at 10, there are so many green and hawksbill turtles. But they're not alone, there are also moray eels, rays stirring as we pass by. This will always be, for us, the Turtle paradise&nbsp;...
