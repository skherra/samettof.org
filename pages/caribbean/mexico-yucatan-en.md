country-desc: |
  **[Cozumel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cozumel){: target="_blank"}** in Mexico was made famous in the 1960s by Commandant Cousteau. The Palancar sites are beautiful spots for fixed marine life, located on the Caribbean's barrier reef. It's a protected area, and it shows&nbsp;: every tropical fish is oversized and there in abundance.

  The **[Yucatán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n){: target="_blank"}** peninsula is packed with dive spots, but the most famous remain the island of Cozumel and the [**cenotes**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cenote){: target="_blank"} between Playa del Carmen and Tulum. Cenotes, or sacred wells, offer breathtaking plays of light and shadow.

  **<i class="fa fa-mail-forward"></i>**{: .accent-color} In Cozumel, the best of&nbsp;: **Palancar Gardens**.

  **<i class="fa fa-mail-forward"></i>**{: .accent-color} From Playa del Carmen, the best of&nbsp;: **El Pit**.

dive-shop: |
  In December 2016.
  {: .visited-in}

  **[Phocéa Cozumel et Playa <i class="fa fa-heart"></i>](http://mexique-plongee.fr/){: target="_blank"}**  is a very large club, it offers several services including airport pickup from Cancún and interesting packages&nbsp;: Cozumel and/or Cenotes. The slightly impersonal welcome is quickly forgotten once underwater, where the guides are passionate and environmentally conscious (briefing on sunscreen and how fragile the corals are).

  It's possible to do 2 dives a day and a night dive on request. The only downside&nbsp;: crowded, with groups of mixed levels, including complete beginners alongside Level 2 divers and above.

  The club closed on Cozumel around 2019.

accommodation: |
  In December 2016.
  {: .visited-in}

  To avoid taking the ferry every morning, it's better to stay on **Cozumel**{: .accent-color}. We didn't enjoy the hotel attached to the dive club, ([Villa blanca](https://villablanca.net/){: target="_blank"}, dated and poorly maintained, poor value for money, especially since there's basically no bar or restaurant). We had excellent breakfasts at the Blue Angel just steps away.

  Plan on a scooter if you're staying far from the club, as there's no pickup. For the cenotes, you can either take the early morning ferry from Cozumel, or stay in the very lively Playa del Carmen. Two ferry companies run between Playa and Cozumel, leaving early in the morning (7am) and getting you back late in the evening (9pm).

      On the way down an eagle ray shows some curiosity, then a nurse shark passes by that we follow from a distance. We descend into the great canyons covered in coral and multicolored sponges&nbsp;: yellow, orange, red. Mazes of caves, where we come across spiny lobsters. Ascending back over the sand, a beautiful green moray and its grouper swim side by side in open water against the current.
  - author: Sam
    place: El Pit
    date: 22/12/2016
    text: |
      A very early dive, we're the first ones at the site; after a 40-minute drive, 10 of them through the jungle, we reach the sinkhole. Small stairs let us climb down and dive into this cenote. The water is very clear, first stalactites, then the halocline between fresh and salt water. Toward the bottom, a cloud of sulfur and pitch black night. 35 m, the atmosphere is surreal, at the start of the tunnel we spot old animal bones. We slowly ascend back toward sea level and play once more with the halocline and its mirages on the rock. A magnificent play of light on the way up to the surface. We'll remember this exceptional atmosphere.
  - author: Sam
    place: Taj Maha
    date: 22/12/2016
    text: |
      Descending once again, alone, into this turquoise pool carved into the rock, a magnificent play of light, then darkness, and our lamps lighting up the cave ceilings. Pretty fossils of sea urchins and conches are visible in the rock. The atmosphere is calm and relaxed, a green light appears, we emerge into a natural pool with magnificent gaps opening onto the jungle.
