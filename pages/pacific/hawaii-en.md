h1: 🇺🇸 Hawaii

country-desc: |
  **[Hawaii](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geography_of_Hawaii){: target="_blank"}**, far from its capital Honolulu, is fascinating, caught between its volcanoes and the Pacific Ocean. The Hawaiian archipelago is still not that well known for diving, but the northern island of **[Kauai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kauai){: target="_blank"}** and the southern **[Big Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawaii_(island)){: target="_blank"}** have some really beautiful dive spots, still with relatively few divers.

  In Kona, on Big Island, the Manta Show remains a very touristy and somewhat questionable attraction because of how it works&nbsp;: it lets you meet a group of about ten oceanic manta rays drawn in by the spotlights.

  On Kauai, dive clubs run a day trip out to Niihau. The dives there are exceptional.

  The Hawaiian islands are reachable by plane, via domestic or international flights from Los Angeles, San Francisco and Papeete.

  **<i class="fa fa-mail-forward"></i>**{: .accent-color} Here's a description of the dives around Kauai&nbsp;: [<i class="fa fa-map"></i>](https://seasportdivers.com/dive-map/){: target="_blank"}.

dive-shop: |
  In May 2016.
  {: .visited-in}

  A cruise around the Kona coast&nbsp;: **[<i class="fa fa-ship"></i> Aggressor](https://www.aggressor.com/destination/Kona){: target="_blank"}** is the only company that operates one and lets you cover Big Island from north to south. Local clubs don't go that far, so you'll be the only divers in the water, aside from at the Kona Show.

  That said, at the time of our stay, the staff was still learning the sites and had major staffing issues (tension among crew members).

  **[Seasport Divers <i class="fa fa-heart"></i>](http://seasportdivers.com/){: target="_blank"}** on Kauai&nbsp;: a really good club based in Poipu, large staff and a comfortable boat. The main draw is the day trip out to Niihau&nbsp;: 3 unforgettable dives, with snorkeling with dolphins possible, in very small groups so as not to scare them off.

accommodation: |
  In May 2016.
  {: .visited-in}

  On Big Island&nbsp;: **[B&B](http://www.makaihale.com/){: target="_blank"}** in northern Big Island, with charming, discreet hosts, a pool and jacuzzi available, and what a view, the volcanoes and the sea,&nbsp;...

  On Kauai&nbsp;: **[Prince Kuhio](http://www.prince-kuhio.com/){: target="_blank"}**&nbsp;: apartment rentals in a lovely complex, right at the edge of Poipu, a stone's throw from the beach. The rooftop apartments are gorgeous&nbsp;!

    What a beautiful dive&nbsp;: first a descent onto the submerged volcano of Niihau, a sheer vertical cliff, gray reef sharks waiting for us. Exploring along the wall with no coral, a strange sight, a curious monk seal joins us above the wall. Then in the distance, a massive silhouette&nbsp;: a hammerhead cruising peacefully by&nbsp;! During the ascent, the intrigued monk seal follows me all the way to the surface, and I stay a while to watch it. On the way back across, we come across two pilot whales&nbsp;!!
