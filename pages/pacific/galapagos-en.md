h1: 🇪🇨 Galápagos

country-desc: |
  The **[Gal&aacute;pagos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gal%C3%A1pagos_Islands){: target="_blank"}** archipelago, in the Pacific, a legendary and otherworldly place, is packed with marine and terrestrial diversity,&nbsp;...

  It remains reachable, a 2-hour flight from the mainland, though with fairly high entrance and on-site fees meant to limit tourism. The Ecuadorian government embraces this to preserve this wild and unique **[heritage site <i class="fa fa-heart"></i>](https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/1/){: target="_blank"}**.

  The diving has an incomparable richness, though visibility can be a bit temperamental,&nbsp;... The most beautiful dives happen around Darwin, Wolf and Fernandina, in fairly cold waters. A cruise is essential to reach these three islands. A stop at Santa Cruz island (next to Baltra, the airport island) makes for a good stopover.

  **[Ecuador](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecuador){: target="_blank"}** is also full of other beautiful regions to visit&nbsp;: the Pacific coast, the Andean region, or the Amazon region,&nbsp;...

  **<i class="fa fa-mail-forward"></i>**{: .accent-color} Here's a map of our itinerary, the best of which&nbsp;: **Darwin Arch, Anchorage,**  and **Cabo Douglas** [<i class="fa fa-map"></i>](/docs/galapagos-map.webp){: target="_blank"}.

dive-shop: |
  In May 2018.
  {: .visited-in}

  **[<i class="fa fa-ship"></i> Aggressor 3 <i class="fa fa-heart"></i>](http://www.aggressor.com/galapagos.php){: target="_blank"}**, with a capacity of 16 people, offers excellent service and 2 passionate dive masters. The boat, very comfortable, departs from Baltra and offers 4 dives a day. The dives are exclusively drift dives from a zodiac.

accommodation: |
  In May 2018.
  {: .visited-in}

  In Guayaquil&nbsp;: **[Ramada Hotel](https://www.hotelramada.com/){: target="_blank"}** for a transit stop in Guayaquil, decent and very well located, facing the Malecón.

  In Puerto Ayora&nbsp;: **[La Casa de Judy <i class="fa fa-heart"></i>](http://www.hotellacasadejudy.com/){: target="_blank"}** a really lovely hotel, quiet, with a view over a canopy, good breakfasts.

  In Cuenca&nbsp;: **Hotel Inés**, a charming, typical hotel in the historic center.

to-see: |
  In Guayaquil, stroll along the **[Malecón](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malec%C3%B3n_2000){: target="_blank"}**, with its beautiful architecture and gardens full of iguanas.

  The Santa Ana lighthouse, in the **Las Peñas** neighborhood.

  In Puerto Ayora, go check out **the church** across from the pier to admire its stained glass windows,

  Not to be missed&nbsp;: the **fish market**, to see sea lions, pelicans, frigatebirds, iguanas and herons around the fishermen,

  The magnificent **Tortuga** beach makes for a beautiful walk.

  Not forgetting the **[Darwin](https://www.darwinfoundation.org/en/){: target="_blank"}** center, really interesting and still active today.

  In Cuenca, the museums and facades of the old town, the **Pumapungo** archaeological site and the **[Ingapirca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ingapirca){: target="_blank"}** site, 60 km from the city.

log-books:
- author: Sam
  place: Darwin
  date: 14/05/2018
  text: |
    A descent under the Darwin Arch, dense schools of barracuda and jacks. We pass over the shark cleaning plateau, several skittish hammerheads swim by quickly, we reach a cavity where we disturb 3 whitetips, on the way out, a Galapagos shark spots us and turns back around, surprised. We leave the reef for the open blue, and at 20 meters, against the white sand, two big schools of hammerheads stand out (more than fifty of them). We stretch out our safety stop to watch them.
- author: Sam
  place: Wolf
  date: 15/05/2018
  text: |
    A descent sheltered by the rocks, we don't wait long before schools of Galapagos and blacktip sharks start moving around us. A hammerhead swims with them. The show lasts 40 minutes, watching them pass around us. There it is&nbsp;: the wall of sharks&nbsp;!
- author: Sam
  place: Cabo Douglas - Fernandina
  date: 15/05/2018
  text: |
    A descent at the time when the iguanas head to the sea to feed, at 10 meters, the sun and the swell make the meadow fascinating, turtles, octopus and sea lions are all part of it. A penguin threads its way between us&nbsp;! The iguanas graze on green algae before slowly climbing back onto their rock in the sun.
- author: Sam
  place: Punta Vicente Roca - Isabella
  date: 15/05/2018
  text: |
    A descent along the rock at 30 meters, 3 big mola molas cruise peacefully in the blue, a few turtles swim close by, then two mischievous sea lions come to play around the divers and startle the mola molas, which vanish in an instant&nbsp;! We played with the sea lions along the wall and a small canyon. Reaching a volcanic sandy area, a turtle cleaning station, electric rays&nbsp;: torpedoes. We're getting cold, we start our ascent.
