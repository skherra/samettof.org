h1: 🇨🇷 Cocos Island

country-desc: |
  **[Cocos Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cocos_Island){: target="_blank"}**, a small paradise for hammerhead sharks and seabirds. It has to be earned, since it takes 36 hours by boat to get there from Punta Arenas, with sometimes very rough seas.

  These waters, on the North Pacific side, are part of the heavily regulated Cocos **[marine park <i class="fa fa-heart"></i>](https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/820/){: target="_blank"}**. The island is uninhabited, a group of rangers, resupplied by the cruise boats, camps there and keeps watch over and protects the park. Every dive from the three cruise boats is scheduled with precision, down to the half hour, no anchors, only mooring buoys. Every dive is a drift dive. This sanctuary remains pristine. Even though visibility can sometimes be temperamental, the show is magical&nbsp;: hammerheads on every single dive&nbsp;!

  Cocos Island, worthy of Jurassic Park, is a humid tropical forest. It's also a sanctuary for frigatebirds, boobies, blue- or red-footed boobies, and white terns called locally "Esperitu Santo".

  Mainland **[Costa Rica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Costa_Rica){: target="_blank"}** is also full of wonders&nbsp;: plants, insects and tropical animals. Environmentalists ahead of their time, this country has been committed to protecting its natural heritage for a very long time. Pura Vida&nbsp;!

  **<i class="fa fa-mail-forward"></i>**{: .accent-color} Here's a description of the dive sites at Cocos, the best of which are&nbsp;: **Manuelita Outside, Alcyone** and **Dirty Rock**&nbsp;: [<i class="fa fa-map"></i>](https://underseahunter.com/cocos-island/#dive-sites){: target="_blank"}.

      A descent to the first cleaning station, a mobula ray passes in front of us, jacks right before our eyes, a rather dense school, which prompts the divemaster to drop a bit further down onto the sandy bottom, and it's a full-on show&nbsp;: two big Galapagos sharks, a tiger shark, stingrays on the bottom. After about twenty minutes, we ascend back onto the reef, a lovely eagle ray glides by in the blue,&nbsp;... And it doesn't stop there, two or three hammerheads above us. To finish off with an oceanic manta ray, at least 6 meters across&nbsp;!! Whoa.

      A roughly thirty-minute watch at the cleaning station at 30 meters. We see numerous hammerheads pass by, a Galapagos shark, small whitetips galore and big jacks (blue, black, silver).

      Second part of the dive as a drift dive out in the blue, where we come across a wall of about a hundred hammerheads, twice.

      Other solitary hammerheads make a few passes during the ascent and at the safety stop.
  - author: Sam
    place: Dirty Rock
    date: 16/09/2019
    text: |
      Last dive at this splendid cleaning station, we hear whales close by, soon several Galapagos sharks and hammerheads honor us with their presence, being cleaned by king angelfish (no less). It's rather cool, 24 degrees, after 20 minutes of a splendid show, whitetips at our feet, moray eels, and above us schools of gray jacks. We leave the spot to move out into open water, and there, a school of nearly a hundred hammerheads in front of us honors us once more. At the safety stop, we're not shortchanged either, a silky shark passes close by, as does a silvertip. A beautiful dive that wraps up 7 days of diving at Cocos.
