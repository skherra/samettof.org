h1: 🇫🇯 Fiji

country-desc: |
  The **[Fiji](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiji){: target="_blank"}** archipelago, in the Pacific, is part of the western Pacific region that includes Papua, New Caledonia and Vanuatu, more commonly known as **[Melanesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melanesia){: target="_blank"}** (a term that remains up for debate among geologists and anthropologists).

  The waters of the **Somosomo** channel plunge us into an Indo-Pacific atmosphere full of life and coral. The Rainbow Reef is absolutely stunning, with its multicolored hard and soft corals. There are more than twenty sites, which you can dive again and again depending on the current's direction. The reefs never drop below 30 meters, except for the Great White Wall. This deep, magnificent dive site is reachable depending on the tides. It's part of the top 10 most beautiful sites in the world, and it lives up to its reputation&nbsp;: it's the only place in Fiji where it "snows"&nbsp;!! Since dive clubs only go on specific dates, usually twice a week, it's worth checking to make sure you land on the right dates,&nbsp;...

  Dives in the **south-southeast of Viti Levu** and **southeast of Vanua Levu** offer a different topography&nbsp;: walls, pinnacles and passes. There's less coral than in the north, and visibility isn't as good, but there are more pelagics. We got to enjoy the sight of 4 humpback whales from the boat.

  The dives are drift dives, and the current can be moderate to strong depending on the tide, but there are dives for every level.

  **<i class="fa fa-mail-forward"></i>**{: .accent-color} Here's the description of the dive sites at Taveuni&nbsp;: [<i class="fa fa-map"></i>](http://www.taveunidiveresort.com/dive-guide#){: target="_blank"}.

dive-shop: |
  In September 2018.
  {: .visited-in}

  **[Taveuni Dive <i class="fa fa-heart"></i>](http://www.taveunidiveresort.com/dive-shop/){: target="_blank"}** has 3 boats at a small marina a 2-minute walk from the resort. The biggest boat can take 9 divers. There are five dive masters. We dived with David and Alfred, two passionate locals. Groups are capped at 5-6 people, we never did the same site twice all week.

  The sites are 15 minutes by boat from the marina. Two dives in the morning starting at 8am, and one offered in the afternoon, though the timing on that one stays tricky, "Fiji Time" <i class="fa fa-smile-o"></i>

  **[<i class="fa fa-ship"></i> Fiji Aggressor](http://www.aggressor.com/){: target="_blank"}**  has a capacity of 10 people, an old boat that isn't particularly well maintained, with an unpleasant smell in the cabins, quite expensive for what's on offer. Only the captain and the dive master save the trip, offering some really beautiful dives depending on the mood and the weather&nbsp;: macro or a good drift dive in the current. 4 to 5 dives a day when the weather allows, from the boat or the zodiac.

  Since 2019, this cruise has been pulled from the Aggressor website.

  In Taveuni&nbsp;: **[Taveuni Dive Resort](http://www.taveunidiveresort.com/){: target="_blank"}**&nbsp;: A really beautiful resort set in a lush garden, opened in 2017, still has room to improve, it's more of a resort than a dive resort. A bit pricey for what you get.

  In Suva&nbsp;: **[Grand Pacific Hotel](http://grandpacifichotel.com.fj/){: target="_blank"}**&nbsp;: A splurge <i class="fa fa-smile-o"></i>, this magnificent hotel is steeped in history, Queen Elizabeth once stayed here. The rooms are large and comfortable. The service is impeccable.

  In Nadi&nbsp;: **Tropic of Capricorn <i class="fa fa-heart"></i>**&nbsp;: a simple backpacker hotel with a magnificent view over Wailoaloa Bay. Lovely staff, small bar where you can order food and drinks.

to-see: |
  In Nadi&nbsp;: **Fiji Essence and Spa**, for a really pleasant transit and rejuvenating massages, 5 minutes from Nadi airport, transfer included.

  In Suva&nbsp;: **[Grand Pacific Hotel](http://grandpacifichotel.com.fj/){: target="_blank"}** for the historic setting, to grab a drink or dinner. One of the only buildings worth seeing in Suva.

  At Gau or Taveuni&nbsp;: Get invited to the village of **Somosomo**, to chat with the villagers, listen to their songs and enjoy delicious local food.

  text: |
    Descent onto the plateau at 12 meters, then through a tube that opens onto the wall at 24 meters. The sight is stunning&nbsp;: a wide-open blue and an endless vertical drop covered in white soft coral (getting denser the deeper you go), a moray eel poking its head out of its hole on the wall amid the white corals.

    After a 20-minute swim along the wall, the second part of the dive is on the plateau, where beautiful light bathes an extremely rich coral reef.
- author: Sam
  place: North Save-a-tack
  date: 11/09/2018
  text: |
    Descent along the wall with the captain, we spot sharks, so we completely change plans and continue along the wall, and there, 3-4 hammerheads out in the blue, 3 whitetips and 1 blacktip, visibility is poor and we struggle to make them out, quite a bit of counter-current to hold our position in the small pass. At the end of the wall, three big, dense schools of jacks, barracudas and bonito.

    Wow, we could be back along the walls of Polynesia&nbsp;!!

    Once in the pass, a small incoming drift dive, we spot a gigantic grouper. With the rest of the group wanting to ascend, and fatigue setting in after our fourth dive of the day, we head back up to the boat.

We stay at the bottom of the canyon to enjoy them, we don't know where to look&nbsp;! But we soon have to get back to the boat, too bad, we would have happily stayed a bit longer,&nbsp;...
