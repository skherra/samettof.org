country-desc: |
  **[Ponta do Ouro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ponta_do_Ouro){: target="_blank"}**  is a beach town in Mozambique, close to the South African border, within the large **[iSimangaliso](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/iSimangaliso_Wetland_Park){: target="_blank"}** wetland reserve. By road it's about 6.5 hours' drive from Durban airport. Crossing the border is easy enough, though there can be a long wait, especially in summer. It's a very popular spot with South Africans, and there are plenty of lodges and dive clubs on site.

  The zodiac launches, with the divers' help, happen from the beach, and you then have to get through the waves, which makes the dives a bit of a workout.

  Two main sites at 40 meters&nbsp;: Atlantis, 5 minutes from the beach, and the Pinacles reef, a 20-minute boat ride from the beach. And out there in the deep blue&nbsp;: hammerheads, bull sharks, tiger sharks, silky sharks, blacktips, rays&nbsp;: it's a real show&nbsp;!

  **<i class="fa fa-mail-forward"></i>**{: .accent-color} Here's the map of the dive sites, the best of which is **Pinacles**&nbsp;: [<i class="fa fa-map"></i>](/docs/mozambique-ponta-reefs-map.webp){: target="_blank"}.

dive-shop: |
  In February 2018.
  {: .visited-in}

  **[Scuba Addicts <i class="fa fa-heart"></i>](http://www.scubaaddicts.com/){: target="_blank"}** offers several South Africa and Mozambique packages. At Ponta, 2 to 3 dives a day.

  Large zodiac, the skipper and guide know the sites and the sea very well, and run safe dives, on the other hand, diving isn't always possible depending on the weather.

  We clearly preferred the Mozambique team.

accommodation: |
  In February 2018.
  {: .visited-in}

  The **[Scuba Addicts](https://www.scubaaddicts.com/scuba-diving-mozambique.html){: target="_blank"}** lodge has a huge terrace, 2 small pools, and a lovely common area with a sea view. The dive club is right inside the lodge.

  Air conditioning is a nice plus against mosquitoes and the heat. The lodge offers light meals on site, and there are several restaurants in the village of Ponta do Ouro depending on the season.

  The hosts live at the lodge, which makes for a warmer welcome.

to-see: |
  Stroll along the pretty beach of **Ponta do Ouro** and Ponta **Malongane**. It's difficult to drive on the tracks even with a 4x4, watch out for soft sand.

    A descent onto a school of hammerheads gently swaying around us, all sizes, these sharks are majestic, we decide to swim against the current to make the most of their presence. Further along the reef, a lovely eagle ray rises to check us out. Then out in the deep blue, a tiger shark trailed by its remoras, circling us for a good 3 minutes at less than 10 meters, we can make out its stripes and its beautiful shape&nbsp;!! The boat ride back to the beach is a challenge in itself, the boat literally takes off from the water to land on the beach&nbsp;<i class="fa fa-smile-o"></i>&nbsp;!!
