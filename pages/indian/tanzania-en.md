h1: 🇹🇿 Tanzania

country-desc: |
  **[Tanzania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzania){: target="_blank"}** is the land of high-end safaris&nbsp;: In the vast **[Serengeti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serengeti_National_Park){: target="_blank"}** park, with its great plains, a paradise for lions&nbsp;; in **[Tarangire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tarangire_National_Park){: target="_blank"}**, the park of elephants and baobabs&nbsp;; around Lake Manyara with its birds and hippos&nbsp;; in the magnificent **[Ngorongoro crater](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ngorongoro_Crater){: target="_blank"}**.

  After several days of feasting our eyes, a short extension on **[Pemba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pemba_Island){: target="_blank"}** island, to rest and dive, let us end this incredible trip in style.

  We settled in near Misali island and dived the reef there, where the seabed is teeming with fish and life. At most, we were 4 or 5 people underwater&nbsp;!

  **<i class="fa fa-mail-forward"></i>**{: .accent-color} Here's the description of the dives around Misali&nbsp;: [<i class="fa fa-map"></i>](http://www.fundulagoon.com/#/ocean/diving){: target="_blank"}.

  text: |
    A beautiful drift dive along the coral wall, the current is fairly strong, we decide to hold on beneath a school of barracudas that don't budge an inch. We soon spot two big Napoleon wrasse, about 2 meters long. Visibility is so clear that you can see the blue and green around their eyes. Then back up onto the reef, and a sandy slope where we spot garden eels.
